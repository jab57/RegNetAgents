## üí° Pro Tips

### 1. Submit to Multiple Conferences
- Acceptance rates vary (30-70% for posters)
- Hedge your bets by submitting to 3-5 conferences
- Diversify across computational and applied biology

### 2. Tailor Your Poster for Each Audience

**ISMB** (Methods focus):
- Emphasize: LLM-powered domain agents, LangGraph architecture, intelligent routing, local inference deployment
- Lead with: Technical innovations combining network analysis with LLM-generated scientific insights
- Highlight: AI-generated scientific rationales, graceful fallback architecture
- Background: Biological applications (gene regulatory networks, biomarker discovery)

**Single Cell Genomics** (Data focus):
- Emphasize: Pre-computed regulatory networks from CellxGene scRNA-seq data, 500K+ cells, ARACNe algorithm, cell-type-specific networks
- Lead with: Single-cell data processing pipeline, network inference methods
- Background: Workflow orchestration for multi-agent analysis

**AACR** (Clinical focus):
- Emphasize: Biomarker discovery, therapeutic targets, clinical utility
- Lead with: Colorectal cancer case study, literature validation
- Background: Computational methods

**NeurIPS** (AI/ML focus):
- Emphasize: LLM-powered multi-agent system, local inference deployment (Ollama), LangGraph orchestration
- Lead with: Novel AI architecture combining graph analysis with LLM-generated domain insights
- Highlight: Graceful fallback design, practical deployment considerations, local inference without API costs
- Background: Domain applications in gene regulatory analysis

**ICSB** (Systems focus):
- Emphasize: Regulatory networks, perturbation analysis, network topology
- Lead with: Systems-level insights, hub regulators
- Background: Implementation details

### 3. Abstract Writing Tips

**Structure**:
1. **Background** (1-2 sentences): Gene regulatory networks, current limitations
2. **Methods** (2-3 sentences): LangGraph multi-agent framework, key innovations
3. **Results** (2-3 sentences): Performance metrics, case study findings
4. **Conclusions** (1-2 sentences): Impact, applications, significance

**Key Phrases to Include**:
- "LLM-powered multi-agent AI framework"
- "Local language model inference (Ollama)"
- "AI-generated scientific rationales"
- "LangGraph workflow orchestration"
- "500K+ single cells"
- "100-200√ó faster than manual literature review"
- "Literature-validated biomarkers"
- "Perturbation analysis for therapeutic target identification"
- "Network topology-based prioritization"
- "Graceful fallback architecture"

**Avoid**:
- Overstating capabilities (no "predicts gene expression")
- Comparison claims against other tools
- Buzzwords without substance

### 4. Timing and Deadlines

**Set Google Alerts** for:
- "ISMB 2026 abstract deadline"
- "Single Cell Genomics 2026 submission"
- "ICSB 2026 call for abstracts"
- Conference names + "2026"

**Typical Timeline**:
- Abstract submission: 3-4 months before conference
- Acceptance notification: 1-2 months before
- Early registration discount: 2-3 months before
- Final poster upload (if required): 1-2 weeks before

### 5. Registration and Funding

**Costs** (estimates):
- Registration: $300-800 (student/postdoc discounts available)
- Travel: $500-2000 depending on location
- Hotel: $150-300/night

**Funding Sources**:
- Conference travel awards (apply when submitting abstract)
- Student/postdoc presentation awards
- Institutional travel grants
- ISCB travel fellowships (for ISMB)

### 6. Networking Strategy

**Before Conference**:
- Identify labs working on: regulatory networks, single-cell analysis, AI in biology
- Email PIs: "I'll be presenting a poster on X, would love to discuss Y"
- Follow relevant researchers on Twitter/X

**During Conference**:
- Stand by your poster during assigned session
- Prepare 2-minute and 5-minute versions of your pitch
- Have business cards or QR code to paper/poster PDF
- Attend talks by groups doing related work

**After Conference**:
- Follow up with interested people within 1 week
- Share poster PDF if requested
- Connect on LinkedIn/Twitter
